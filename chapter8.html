Chapter 8: Information Security and Cyber Law

Information security involves protecting data from unauthorized access, theft, and corruption. Cyber law refers to the legal aspects related to the use of digital technology and the internet.

Key Concepts of Information Security:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that data is only accessible by authorized individuals.
  • Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data over its lifecycle.
  • Availability: Ensuring that data and systems are available when needed by authorized users.

Common Threats to Information Security:

  • Malware: Software designed to damage or disrupt systems.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information through deceptive means.
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access to confidential information.

Cyber Law:

Cyber law covers legal issues related to the use of the internet, data protection, intellectual property, and online transactions. It includes laws regarding hacking, data privacy, and digital signatures.
